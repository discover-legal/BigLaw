## Contents Overview

### 1. Executive Summary
Comprehensive overview of all six allegation categories against Whitmore Capital Advisors LLC, its CEO Gerald R. Whitmore, Managing Director Diana L. Chao, and Vice President Marcus T. Bellini. Total estimated client harm exceeds $8.6 million.

### 2. Allegation Category 1: Cherry-Picking Trade Allocations
**Statutory Violations:** Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act

**Key Evidence:**
- 5,776 omnibus equity trades (73% of total trading volume) analyzed
- 4,217 trades with complete allocation records reviewed by SEC Office of Quantitative Research
- Oceanic Fund I LP received 68.4% profitable allocations vs. 41.2% for other accounts
- 27.2 percentage point disparity (statistically significant: t-statistic = 4.73; p < 0.001)
- Chao's personal account: 81.6% profitable allocation rate (71 of 87 allocations)
- Estimated excess profits: $8,238,000 ($7.8M to Oceanic Fund I LP + $438K to Chao's account)

**Motivation:** Whitmore's 12% limited partnership interest in Oceanic Fund I LP ($22.2 million value) and 62% membership interest in WCA (earning management fees and performance-based compensation)

### 3. Allegation Category 2: Misleading Form ADV Disclosures
**Statutory Violations:** Section 207 of the Investment Advisers Act; Rule 206(4)-1(a)(5)

**Misstatements and Omissions:**
- **Item 6:** Represented trades allocated "pro rata" with "no preferential treatment" — contradicted by 27.2 pp disparity
- **Item 11:** Failed to disclose Whitmore's 12% LP interest in Oceanic Fund I LP ($22.2M value)
- **Item 11:** Failed to disclose Chao's personal trading account receiving omnibus allocations
- **Item 12:** Failed to disclose Lakeshore Trading arrangement and Bellini's relationship with Ostrowski

**Impact:** Omissions deprived clients of material information necessary to evaluate allocation practices

### 4. Allegation Category 3: Deficient Written Policies and Procedures
**Statutory Violations:** Rule 206(4)-7 under the Investment Advisers Act

**Critical Deficiencies:**
1. **Omnibus Trade Allocation Procedures:** Section 7.3 of Compliance Manual addressed IPO/secondary offerings but was completely silent on omnibus trade allocations (73% of trading volume)
2. **Employee Omnibus Allocations:** Section 9.1 did not restrict employees from receiving omnibus allocations in personal accounts (allowed Chao to receive 87 allocations without pre-clearance)
3. **Inadequate Annual Compliance Review:** Hargrove & Tilton review (Dec. 8, 2023) did not include quantitative testing of allocation patterns

### 5. Allegation Category 4: Failure to Maintain Required Books and Records
**Statutory Violations:** Rule 204-2(a)(3) and Rule 204-2(a)(7)

**Recordkeeping Failures:**
1. **Missing Records:** 2,614 of 5,776 omnibus trades (45.2%) had no contemporaneous allocation records
2. **Backdated Records:** 312 allocation spreadsheets had metadata showing creation dates averaging 17 business days after trade dates (some exceeded 30 days)
3. **Implication:** Backdating suggests post-hoc allocation decisions made after observing closing prices

### 6. Allegation Category 5: Breach of Fiduciary Duty / Directed Brokerage
**Statutory Violations:** Sections 206(1) and 206(2) of the Investment Advisers Act

**The Arrangement:**
- **Period:** June 2022 – November 2023 (18 months)
- **Trades:** 23 block trades totaling $48.7 million (9.4 million shares)
- **Broker:** Lakeshore Trading LLC (registered broker-dealer, FINRA member)
- **Personal Relationship:** Bellini and Ostrowski (40% owner of Lakeshore Trading) were college roommates at University of Virginia (1999-2003)
- **Undisclosed:** Relationship not disclosed to WCA compliance, Whitmore, or clients

**Financial Impact:**
- **Commission Disparity:** Lakeshore Trading charged 4.2¢/share vs. 1.1¢/share for comparable trades
- **Excess Commissions:** $291,400 total ($187,000 borne by Crescent Bay Municipal Pension Fund)
- **Undisclosed Compensation to Bellini:** $92,600 (Ostrowski paid Bayshore Palms Golf & Country Club: $45,000 initiation fee + $47,600 in monthly dues for 17 months)

### 7. Allegation Category 6: Obstructive Conduct During Examination
**Statutory Violations:** Section 209(e) of the Investment Advisers Act

**The Conduct:**
- **Date:** April 22, 2024
- **Context:** SEC examination commenced March 11, 2024; document requests for allocation records already served
- **Action:** Whitmore instructed IT Administrator Tomás Delgado to "clean up the shared drive" by deleting folders containing trade allocation spreadsheets from 2021 and early 2022
- **Evidence:** Email from Whitmore to Delgado instructing removal of "old allocation folders from Q1 2021 through Q1 2022"
- **Forensic Recovery:** SEC Office of Information Technology recovered 47 deleted files containing trade allocation spreadsheets from January 2021 through March 2022

### 8. Cross-Document Analysis and Corroboration

**Interconnections Identified:**
1. **Deficient Policies Enable Cherry-Picking:** Absence of omnibus allocation procedures (Allegation 3) directly enabled cherry-picking scheme (Allegation 1)
2. **Recordkeeping Failures Conceal Cherry-Picking:** Missing and backdated records (Allegation 4) facilitated cherry-picking (Allegation 1)
3. **Misleading Disclosures Conceal Conflicts:** Form ADV omissions (Allegation 2) concealed conflicts motivating cherry-picking (Allegation 1)
4. **Obstructive Conduct Destroys Evidence:** Record deletion (Allegation 6) destroyed evidence of recordkeeping failures (Allegation 4) and cherry-picking (Allegation 1)

**Corroborating Documents:**
- Hargrove & Tilton Compliance Review (Dec. 8, 2023): Identified gaps in omnibus allocation procedures and employee omnibus allocation restrictions
- Employee Trading Log and Pre-Clearance Records: Revealed gap in disclosure of Chao's Meridian account
- Ostrowski Bank Records: Direct documentary evidence of $92,600 in undisclosed compensation to Bellini
- SEC Quantitative Analysis: Rigorous statistical corroboration of cherry-picking with t-statistic = 4.73 (p < 0.001)

## Key Findings

### Strengths of SEC's Case
1. **Quantitative Evidence:** Robust statistical analysis with t-statistic = 4.73 (p < 0.001)
2. **Documentary Evidence:** Email instruction to delete records; forensic recovery of deleted files
3. **Financial Motive:** Clear conflict of interest (Whitmore's $22.2M LP interest in Oceanic Fund I LP)
4. **Corroborating Evidence:** Bank records, backdated spreadsheets, missing allocation records
5. **Systematic Pattern:** Consistent disparity across all three years of Review Period (2021-2023)

### Weaknesses of Defense
1. **Overwhelming Quantitative Evidence:** 27.2 percentage point disparity is substantial and statistically significant
2. **Obstructive Conduct:** Deletion of records during examination suggests consciousness of guilt
3. **Undisclosed Conflicts:** Multiple material omissions from Form ADV
4. **Deficient Compliance:** Absence of omnibus allocation procedures in Compliance Manual
5. **Undisclosed Compensation:** Bank records directly document $92,600 paid to Bellini

## Conclusion

The SEC's enforcement referral presents a comprehensive and well-documented case of systematic misconduct. The allegations are supported by robust quantitative evidence, direct documentary evidence of obstructive conduct, bank records documenting undisclosed compensation, forensic evidence of backdated records, and material omissions from regulatory filings.

The defense faces significant challenges. Settlement negotiations may be the most prudent course of action, but any settlement will likely require substantial disgorgement, civil penalties, and industry bars for the named individuals.
